About me..

Hi, I am Jitesh Shah. My native place is Pune, although I am currently located in the South Bay area.

By career, I am a software engineer working in the area of (but not limited to) embedded systems. My alma mater is Pune Institute of Computer Technology (Batch of 2008). There on I worked a couple of years at Marvell India Pvt. Ltd . I joined NCSU for Masters in Computer Science in Fall 2010 and graduated Summer of 2012. While at NCSU, I was part of the Cyber Defense Laboratory with Dr. Peng Ning as my advisor. My Masters thesis was to write a bare-metal Operating System running the ARM Trustzone world featuring periodic as well as event-based triggers. Find the thesis report here. I interned in the Tegra5 team at NVIDIA in the summer of 2011. As of early 2014, I am working as a Senior Software Engineer at Samsung Telecommunication America's research labs. My primary job description being system security. I am work on bootloader/kernel security using (but not limited to) ARM trustzone and ARM hypervisor extensions.

Technically I work in low-level systems, mostly Linux-based. Following are the links to some of the projects I've worked on over the years: Optimizing TCP/IP communication in a virtual network (2008, draft of the paper), A novel network-dependent TCP congestion control procotol (2010) , Enable cloud customers to trust the cloud (2011), Offline patching of VMs in a cloud environment (2011). Besides I've worked extensively on the ARM architecture including SIMD programming, optimizing glibc implementation for targeted hardware, TrustZone and hypevisor extensions for armv7. In summary, my interests are in two diverse fields: Cloud Computing and Embedded Systems.

I am a part of Dreamz Group (a group of tech-savvy passion-driven professionals who mentor awesome undergraduate final year projects) and NCSU LUG. I write a technical blog. Admittedly, it is very unstructured. I use it to dump any notes and handy tricks I come across.

Apart from that I play Counter-Strike quite well. Its my way to bust stress and refresh after a long day. I am quite good at Ping Pong too. I own a Kindle and love reading fiction in my spare time.
