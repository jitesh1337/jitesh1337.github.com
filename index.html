About me..

Hi, I am Jitesh Shah. My native place is Pune, although I am currently located at Raleigh, USA.

By career, I am a software engineer working mostly in the area of (but not limited to) embedded systems. My alma matter is Pune Institute of Computer Technology (Batch of 2008). There on I worked a couple of years at Marvell India Pvt. Ltd . As of early 2012, I am a Masters Thesis student at North Carolina State Univeristy. My thesis topic revolves around ARM's TrustZone security extensions. I interned in the Tegra5 team at NVIDIA and planning to join the same team later in 2012 as a full-time employee.

Technically I work in low-level systems, mostly Linux. Following are links to some of the project I've worked on over the years: Optimizing TCP/IP communication in a virtual network (2008, draft of the paper), A novel network-dependent TCP congestion control procotol (2010) , Enable cloud customers to trust the cloud (2011), Offline patching of VMs in a cloud environment (2011). Besides I've worked extensively on the ARM architecture including SIMD programming, optimizing glibc implementation for targeted hardware and TrustZone. In summary, my interests are in two diverse fields: Cloud Computing and Embedded Systems.

I am a part of Dreamz Group (a group of tech-savvy passion-driven professionals who mentor awesome undergraduate final year projects) and NCSU LUG. I write a technical blog. Admittedly, it is very unstructured. I use it to dump any notes and handy tricks I come across.

Apart from that I am a die-hard Counter-Strike fan. Its my way to bust stress and refresh after a long day. I am quite good at Ping Pong too. I own a Kindle and love reading fiction in my spare time. My latest book was Joseph Heller's Catch-22. I like cooking and always in a for pot-luck night! Religiously, I am Jain, albeit a confused one.
